My Draft Learning Plan

Long Term Goals

  • I want to work somewhere that aligns with my personal values. A sustainable startup, charity or not-for-profit would suit me well. I have been learning a lot recently about how much help our environment really needs and I can’t think of anything else more important than helping that cause.
  • I want to be stable enough to get a dog and ideally take it with me to work as well.
  • I want to continue learning every day. I chose Web Development because I know there will be problems to solve every single day and I look forward to the exciting new challenges.

Non-tech skills I would like to develop

  • I would like to learn how to act in a professional setting. I have only ever had jobs where management is extremely laid back and relaxed and have never been in anything remotely close to an office job.
  • Networking - During my day in the life experience Rohan described tech in NZ as “nepotism on steroids”. I’d like to learn more effective ways to network and make connections.
  • Time management - I blame shift work for making me only productive for half a day at a time. I need to bust these habits and this mindset and learn to manage my time more effectively.

Strengths and limitations

Strengths

  • Courage
  • Curiosity
  • Empathy

    • Weaknesses

    • Anxiety
    • Discipline
    I think one of my biggest assets is my courage. It’s an interesting one because I am also a very anxious person by nature and tend to worry a lot everything both little and big (one of my limitations) - but because I worry about things no matter their size, and that I have to do anyway, it almost offsets the same worry that I feel for big leaps and because I am used to “feeling the fear and doing it anyway” I can push past it and take on much larger challenges than what is expected of me. For example i’ll be just as nervous about ordering takeaways on the phone as I am about making a speech in front of 50 people, or buying a one way ticket to africa by myself... It’s quite a strange way to live but it is working for me so far and has lead me to do some incredible things that would only be possible with big leaps.

    My commitment

    I am fully committed to EDA and will do everything I can to ensure success both in my own learning and that of my peers. I will tackle my learning week-by-week and one step at a time, always breaking bigger problems down into more manageable ones. I’ll work with my cohort on common problems and work on my communication skills throughout the course. When I get stuck on a coding problem I will first try and fully understand what the problem really is. Then i’ll break it down into smaller parts and google them to see what I can find. If google isn’t working i’ll reach out to the slack channels if i’m not on campus or to a teacher or other student on campus if i’m there. If I come across a non-tech problem (life problem?) I’ll contact Allie or another member of the facilitation team as soon as possible if I think it will affect my learning. I expect the facilitation team to be approachable, dependable and understanding.

    Scheduling

    I don’t have any block-out times however I am definitely a morning person and am much more productive in the morning than the afternoon. I also have boxing at 6pm on Mondays and Thursdays. These are optional but I do feel like they are hugely beneficial and I always have a much better week when I attend so ideally will leave campus in time to get to those sessions.
