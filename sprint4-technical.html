Sprint-4 technical

Problems!

I got 99 problems and coding is almost all of them. This was a really tough sprint for me. The first two kata’s tricked me into a false sense of security and from 3 onwards I found them really hard and there was no longer that sense of achievement coming from doing things right because it seemed like everything I tried was wrong (despite doing the research!). When I got really stuck I googled things which worked maybe 20% of the time and when that wouldn’t work i’d have a break and do something non-coding related then get back to it. I found breaking problems into smaller ones was the best method for me to get all the pieces together, but I still struggled putting all of those pieces together into working code. In any case there is a lot of room for improvement so watch this space!

Problem solving techniques:

Pseudocode

Pseudocode really helps break down the problem into smaller ones and is something I should have tried harder to do more of.

Trying something

Almost everything I tried didn’t work but I know I will feel more confident trying things as my base knowledge expands over the next 12 weeks.

Rubber ducky method

I LOVE this method. Anyone that knows me knows how much I love a good rubber duck. I already had one on my desk at home so this was a great technique for me and I felt very confident using it. Whether it be my rubber ducky, my boyfriend, my houseplants or my flatmates, I was constantly talking about the problems that I was facing during this sprint which really helped me get my head around them.

Reading error messages

At the start the error messages were as clear as mud to me but I am getting more confident reading them and am finding them easier and easier to decipher.

Console.logging

I haven’t been doing this for long but I have been finding it really helpful to test out functions and to get more information - I found it especially helpful in the where are you challenge… although I am still yet to have working code for this it gave me useful information about the arrays.

Googling

Google and I have grown up together. We were actually created only months apart so I actually can’t remember a world without it! So of course I find it an incredibly useful resource. I’ve been particularly enjoying youtube videos to avoid excessive walls of text as well.

Asking your peers for help

I have depended on my cohort a lot this week. Even just keeping each other on track has been really good. I feel confident asking them for help and know that it benefits them as well to explain things another way.

Asking coaches for help

This is something I should have been doing earlier. I probably haven’t been doing this enough but i’m sure I will depend on them more and more as the weeks go on.

Improving your process with reflection

I haven’t been confident enough in my code yet to improve it too much once finished but I do find reflection has helped me pinpoint where I went wrong / got distracted / didn’t do enough. I have also started reflecting on each day writing every night about ways I looked after myself and things that I am grateful for and I have found this really rounds the days off nicely and has me sleeping better doing something not coding related right before bed.
